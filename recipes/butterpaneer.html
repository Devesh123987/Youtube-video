Butter Paneer

Description

Paneer makhani (also called butter paneer) is a slightly sweet Indian dish of paneer, originating in New Delhi, in which the gravy is prepared usually with butter (makhan), tomatoes and cashews.

It stimulates hemoglobin production and also aids in building a strong immune system. Not just this, the Vitamin B complex present in paneer is highly essential for growing children. It helps in improving concentration and also enhances memory in kids.

Ingredients

  • 200 grams paneer (cottage cheese)
  • 3 onions
  • 3 tomatoes
  • 10 Cashew nuts
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 4 - 5 pods garlic
  • 1/2 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon kashmiri chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3 cloves
  • 3 cardamom
  • 1 inch cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi leaves (dry fenugreek)
  • 2 - 3 sprigs coriander leaves
  • To taste salt
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons fresh cream

Steps

  1. Firstly, in a large Kadai heat 2 tbsp oil and 2 tbsp butter.
  2. Now add 1 onion and saute until onions turn golden brown.
  3. Keeping the flame on low, add ½ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp chilli powder, ¾ tsp coriander powder, ¼ tsp cumin powder, ½ garam masala and 1 tsp salt.
  4. Saute until the spices turn aromatic. Make sure not to burn the spices.
  5. Add in prepared onion tomato paste and mix well.
  6. Cook until the oil separates from the masala paste.
  7. Now add 1 cup water and mix well adjusting the consistency.
  8. Further, add 2 tbsp cream and mix well.
  9. Also, add 16 cubes of paneer and mix gently.
  10. Simmer for 5 minutes, or until the paneer absorbs the flavour.
  11. Add 1 tsp kasuri methi and 2 tbsp coriander. Mix well.
  12. Finally, enjoy paneer butter masala with roti or naan.
